EU SMEs in China — where they cluster

Documented presence of European small and medium-sized enterprises across mainland China, drawn from the EU SME Centre & European Chamber 2025/26 survey, national chamber networks and city-level records such as Taicang's German enterprise registry.

Scope note: no public registry enumerates every EU SME operating in China — neither Chinese corporate registries nor EU bodies publish a complete, company-level list. This dashboard maps what is documented: survey-reported city clusters, published chamber-network sizes by country of origin, the one city with an official company count (Taicang), and the directories where individual firms can be looked up. Sources are linked at the bottom.

Methodology & sources

  • City tiers follow the EU SME Centre / European Union Chamber of Commerce in China 2025/26 Inter-Chamber SME survey key findings (n = 95; 53% SMEs): significant presence in Shanghai, Beijing, Nanjing and Guangzhou; smaller clusters in Tianjin, Shenzhen, Shenyang, Chengdu and Chongqing.
  • Taicang: 550+ German enterprises (500th arrived January 2024), ≈US$6 billion cumulative German investment, sole city designated a Sino-German Enterprise Cooperation Base — Xinhua, People's Daily.
  • Country networks: AHK Greater China (2,700+ members); CCI France Chine (≈2,100 French subsidiaries); China-Italy Chamber (850+ network); Advantage Austria (650+ Austrian subsidiaries); Embassy of Sweden (600+ Sweden-affiliated firms operating); Spanish Chamber estimate (400+ Spanish companies); Benelux Chamber (≈350 member organisations); China Office of Finnish Industries (≈250 Finnish firms, per China Briefing); Danish Trade Council survey 2024 (178 Danish companies identified).
  • Chamber footprints per city: European Chamber chapters (Beijing, Shanghai, Nanjing, Tianjin, Shenyang, South China = Guangzhou/Shenzhen, Southwest = Chengdu/Chongqing); AHK offices Beijing/Shanghai/Guangzhou; CCIFC offices incl. Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen; CICC offices Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Suzhou, Chengdu, Chongqing; BenCham chapters Beijing/Shanghai/Guangzhou; Danish Chamber three regional chapters; Spanish Chamber offices Beijing/Shanghai/South China.
  • Basemap: Natural Earth (via world-atlas), Mercator projection. Country shapes are rendered as geographic context only.
Data retrieved July 2026. Figures are the latest published by each source and are indicative of scale, not a census of all EU SMEs in China.
