Pre-contract: Know Your Debtor
[Speaker Note:]
We are moving from identity → disputes → enforcement outcomes
Check – NECIPS
General Info（Who am I doing business with?): unified social credit code, company name, shareholders, key personnel, change history, branches, annual reports, administrative license
Financial Info (Will they be able to pay?): paid-in capital, mortgage/ pledge registration, administrative penalties, business abnormal operations, seriously illegal and dishonest entity list, liquidation/deregistration, capital reduction
[Speaker Note:]
This reflects current corporate status and regulatory risk signals. From the very beginning, you must know the identity of your counterparty, especially the Chinese (company) name, unified social credit code (serving as the ID number of the company) to conduct background check.
Check – China Judgments Online
Published effective judgments, rulings, and decisions
Shows whether the company has been involved in disputes (as plaintiff or defendant)
May indicate recurring contractual disputes or litigation patterns
[Speaker Note:]
Some cases may not be disclosed due to state secrets, commercial confidentiality, or personal privacy, and judgment/verdict uploads may be subject to delays.
A judgment alone does not indicate bad faith or insolvency
It is a behavioral history database, not a credit scoring system
Check – Enforcement Information Disclosure Website
What it shows: Debtor Name, court, case number, enforcement amount, status of enforcement.
Key enforcement indicators: Restricted high consumption status; Being listed as “Defaulter”; Execution Status; Conclusion of the Current Enforcement Proceedings.
[Speaker Note]:
Enforcement records indicate that the debtor failed to perform the court judgment, and the creditor has applied the execution for the recovery.
Restricted high consumption status VS. Listed as Defaulters (“dishonest
persons subject to enforcement”)
What does “Execution Status: Conclusion of the Current Enforcement Proceedings” indicate? It refers to the situation in which, during the enforcement of a case, the court has exhausted all statutory investigative measures but has not found any property of the judgment debtor that is available for enforcement, or the property discovered cannot be disposed of temporarily. In such cases, the court rules to temporarily conclude the current enforcement proceeding. This does not signify a permanent termination of the case, but rather a procedural and interim method of closing the case at that stage.
Contract Execution: Structuring for Enforceability
[Speaker Note:] Once the correct counterparty has been identified, the next question is whether the contract can be effectively enforced against that entity
How contract execution impacts enforceability
[Speaker Note:] The most common way to execute a contract with a Chinese company is by affixing its company seal or special contract seal.
Payment terms that work in China context
Payment structure determines how easily a claim can be evidenced and enforced
Clear, staged payment obligations (advance / milestone / delivery-based) reduce dispute scope
[Speaker Note:] Not upon “acceptance” because the debtor may deliberately avoid or delay acceptance
Simpler payment logic improves enforceability in court or arbitration
Avoid back-to-back payment structures
Limited visibility on whether upstream payment has been made
Payment depends on third-party performance outside contractual control
Disputes between third party and counterparty may block recovery or delay payment
Payment security mechanisms
Deposit / advance payment: Secures part of the transaction value upfront and reduces exposure before performance starts
Letter of Credit (L/C): Substitutes buyer credit risk with bank payment obligation, subject to compliant shipping and transaction documents
Guarantees (parent company / bank guarantee / personal guarantee): Expands the pool of liable parties beyond the direct contracting entity, increasing enforceability of payment claims
Dispute resolution clause impact on recovery path
Increase of contract dispute court cases
In 2025, Courts nationwide accepted approximately 37.49 million trial and enforcement matters in 2025, an increase of 10.8% year-on-year.
In major cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, courts are heavily burdened with high caseloads, resulting in long procedural timelines. In practice, it typically takes at least six months from case filing to the first hearing. In addition, foreign-related cases are not subject to standard statutory trial time limits, meaning they are not bound by strict statutory deadlines for conclusion.
The current common practice after case filing is initial referral to court-affiliated mediation institutions. If mediation fails, the case will proceed to formal court scheduling and hearing.
Note: Courts are overloaded with cases. Dismissal of cases will relieve the workload of the judge!
Which Local Chinese Court can have Jurisdiction
Rules on Chinese court competence:
Where defendant or plaintiff domiciles;
Where the contract was signed;
Where the contract was performed;
Where the contract object located; or other places connected with the contract.
According to Article 35 of the Civil Procedural Law, if the parties agree to the exclusive jurisdiction of a court that is not stipulated in Civil Procedure Law that exclusive forum clause is invalid.
NOTE: Exclusive jurisdiction of Chinese court might only valid if the court location has a connection with the contract (mandatory rules might apply in harbor, real estate and disputes involving Sino-foreign equity/cooperative joint ventures).
Alternative to Local Court is Arbitration in China
NOTE: Awards to be enforced by Chinese local courts!! But: CIETAC and  BAC are Chinese arbitration commissions. BAC is set up in 1995 following  the introduction of the Chinese Arbitration Law in 1994.
During Performance: Evidence Preservation
Evidence in Chinese civil litigation: core principles
Courts primarily rely on documentary evidence rather than oral statements
Authenticity and original form carry decisive evidentiary weight
Parties bear the burden of preserving and presenting evidence
Types of evidence & admissibility rules
Documentary evidence
Originals have strongest evidentiary value (Civil Procedure Law Art. 73)
Copies may be admitted but have limited probative value if authenticity is disputed
Electronic evidence
Emails, WeChat messages, and electronic records are admissible
Original storage medium should be preserved (SPC evidence rules Art. 14–15)
Witness evidence
Witness testimony is admissible but generally secondary to documentary evidence
Witnesses may appear in court or submit written/video testimony under exceptions
Language and translation requirements
Foreign-language evidence must be accompanied by Chinese translation
Courts require certified translation in cases
Practical enforcement reality
How evidence is evaluated in practice
Courts generally give stronger weight to contemporaneous written evidence
Parties often deny facts not supported by original documents
Judicial preference is for objective documentary evidence over oral statements
Judicial support mechanisms
Parties may apply for court evidence collection
Courts may issue investigation orders or collect evidence ex officio

Alternative / parallel recovery channel:
third-party debt collection
Legal permissibility in China
Chinese law does not prohibit creditors from appointing a third party to collect commercial debts in legal ways (e.g. Payment reminders, negotiation with debtors, accounts receivable management)
China has not established a unified legal and regulatory framework governing commercial debt collection.
“Qualified” service providers
Licensing requirements?
Currently, there is no special entry for commercial debt collection agencies or official "debt collection licence" for general commercial debt collection activities.
A "qualified" collection agency in China generally refers to a company that:
is legally established and operating;
has an appropriate business scope (e.g., accounts receivable management, credit management or business consulting); and
is duly authorized by the creditor.

Spot the risks
Appointing a third-party collection agency is generally lawful.
Due to the absence of a unified regulatory framework, there may be some risks associated with such outsourced collection activities:
The creditor has limited practical control over the agency's day-to-day collection activities.
If the agency engages in unlawful collection practices (e.g., harassment, intimidation, misuse of personal information or other improper means), the creditor may become involved in:
- customer complaints;
- regulatory investigations;
- civil disputes; and
- reputational damage.
Observations
Pre-contract
Identify the debtor’s name and ID / unified social credit code.
Precontract check on the debtor and select a reliable and financially sound contract party to avoid late payments. Call with parties in the industry on debtor’s payment reputation.
Check not only corporate registration, but also litigation and enforcement history.
Pay particular attention to:
repeated contract disputes;
enforcement records;
high-consumption restrictions and dishonest debtor listings.
Contract Structuring and Execution
Avoid payment terms as: “open account” / “payment against documents” / “payment against acceptance” (debtor can refuse to honor payments on any grounds and renegotiate price. Consider interposing one of Chinese window companies if the contract sum is substantial and Chinese contract party is private SME (order > CNY 5,000,000 / commission 1-2%).
Agree on detail payment terms: prepayment / down payment / progress payment / currency risk / tax liability in China & outside China.
Obtain additional payment security whenever possible (deposit, L/C or guarantee)
Check authorization of signatory and insist the agreement is chopped. Ask an original contract copy.
Agree on a dispute settlement by a Chinese court that is not located at the place of the debtor. Or subject dispute settlement to Chinese arbitration. If both parties are located in China BAC is a valid option. Interim measures such as freezing the debtor’s bank accounts can be applied for through the court and are a powerful tool.
Contract management should preferably be part of a salespersons training and bonus scheme incentives.
During Performance
Preserve original documents and electronic records throughout contract performance.
Confirm important matters in writing rather than relying on oral communications.
Retain complete email, WeChat and document trails.
After Default
Act promptly once payment becomes overdue.
Combine commercial negotiations with legal preparation.
Consider litigation or arbitration early where voluntary payment is unlikely.
