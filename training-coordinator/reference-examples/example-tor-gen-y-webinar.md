I. Background
The overall objective of the EU SME Centre is to contribute to improving the trade and economic relations between the EU and China and to further Europe’s interests vis-à-vis the PRC. The EU SME Centre will achieve this by helping EU SMEs overcome barriers and constraints they face on their efforts to invest in or export to the Chinese market.
II. Specific Activity Background
The Chinese market presents one of the world's most dynamic and complex consumer landscape. Segmenting such a massive population, living in a continent size, is not only impossible but also not relevant for an EU SME. Considering the much lower purchasing power of rural households, the focus needs to be on urban areas, where are living most of the potential consumers of imported products.
Keeping in mind that even in urban areas not everyone will be able/willing to buy imported goods, knowing the main groups of consumers will help design a sound marketing/communication strategy.
This webinar is part of a series examining the four groups of urban consumers, this time focusing on Gen Y who were born between 1980 and 1994.  This group consists of approximately 160 people. As the first generation to grow up during China’s rapid economic opening, they are primary drivers of the “new consumption” wave. They are highly educated, digitally native, and have a strong preference for quality, authenticity, and brand storytelling.
This free event will be held in English. It is open to all interested SMEs from EU Member States and countries participating in the Single Market Programme.
III. Activity Summary
This webinar will first describe the characteristics of the “Urban Gen Y” population, with some demographic data, with some demographic data, their spatial distribution, purchasing power. It will then go into the different traits that make them different (or not) from the other groups in China, but also from their European counterparts. How do they spend their money, recent consumption trends lead by changing lifestyle (wellness, self-improvement…). It will then present some ways to sell to them (channels, messaging…)
